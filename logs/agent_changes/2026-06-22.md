What changed:
- Updated the blur application in the notebook to use reflective padding before FFT convolution.
- Regenerated the noisy blurred image outputs after the boundary-handling change.

Why it changed:
- Reflective padding reduces artificial wrap-around artifacts at image boundaries compared with plain circular FFT convolution.

Expected benefit:
- Produces more realistic blurred observations and cleaner boundary behavior for later deblurring experiments.

Risk / side effects:
- Results from before and after this commit are not directly comparable because the boundary condition changed.
- The deblurring methods should use or account for the same boundary assumption when comparing against these generated images.
